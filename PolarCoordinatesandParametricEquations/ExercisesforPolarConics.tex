\begin{problem}
$x^2+2xy+y^2 -x\sqrt{2}+y\sqrt{2} -6= 0$

\begin{solution}

$x^2+2xy+y^2 -x\sqrt{2}+y\sqrt{2} -6= 0$ becomes $(x')^2 = -(y'-3)$  after rotating counter-clockwise through $\theta = \frac{\pi}{4}$.
